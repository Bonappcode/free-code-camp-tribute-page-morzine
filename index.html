Morzine-Avoriaz

A tribute to a one of a kind ski resort town

These awesome twin resort towns and their wonderful inhabitants have changed how I view my life and how I would like to live it. Morzine and its people have inspired me to make myself happier. It wasn't until I arrived in Morzine I realised how much more I was missing out on around the world. Having always dreamt there could be more outside of the grey Northern English mill town I grew up in. Believing it to be out of my reach these inspiring people, the daunting geography and the two very different but equally brilliant towns have shown me what can be done with a little hard work and determination. This is why my first project on the road to becoming a digital nomad (the dream) should I think definitely be a tribute to Morzine!

I would like to continue with a brief timeline of how Morzine has become the incredible town it is today, a town that can inspire you!

1090s

The Ste-Marie-d'Aulps is formed.

The Aulps Abbey was founded around 1093 by monks that came from the Molesne abbey in Burgundy. After the death Guarinus the abbey became a pilgrimage because his relics were held there.

1180s

what's thought to be the original site

Just under a century later the first mentions of Morzine (Latin Margenes, or "border area") appear in the Aulps Abbey records as a Monastic Grange.The granges were manned by lay-brothers, who cultivated the lands and reared livestock.

1530s

the two churhes in Saint Jean d'Aulps

Around this time Saint-Jean d'Aulps (the closest commune to Aulps Abbey) gained independence from the Cistercian monks. For the next 250 years Vall√©e d'Aulps (the small natural area from La Vernaz to Morzine) prospered as a peaceful agricultural collection of villages.

1800s

miners working hard in the workshop

This was the time technology was evolving which led to the area becoming a hub of thriving slate mines. Supplying other local towns and villages such as Thonon and Taninges with huge sheets of stone.

1900s

The Grand Hotel in Morzine

Tourism starts to grow. The first hotel was opened in 1925 by Francois Baud, called The Grand Hotel, it is still in use today as l'haututiere another hotel. Around ten years later he commissioned and built the Pleney Cable Car, only the second to be built in the whole of France.

1940s

pleney cable cars bottom station

At this point the development of skiing in Morzine has to take a back seat. Used as smuggling route during World War Two into France by the French resistance and also as an escape out of France for Jewish people. This lead to Morzine eventually being occupied by the Nazis and then later the department of Haute-Savoie liberating itself. I encourage you to delve further into the local history at this time, a great place to start is a blog by Jackie Becker

1960s

Jean Vuarnet won Gold at the Olympics

After 15 years the ski industry in Morzine had seen a slight resurrection. After Jean Vuarnet won gold at 1960 Squaw Valley Olympics in California and saw his home town of Morzine rally behind him and so he then became crucial in the development of Avoriaz, linking up local resorts to what we now know as Portes du Soleil.

2021...

MORZINE!

Today Morzine is a vibrant bustling and exciting sporting destination for travellers from around the world, offering both winter and summer activities.

Morzine has inspired me to become a digital nomad, to earn money whilst I am traveling, finding new amazing peope and places. Morzine seems worlds away from my hometown, a grim Northern England ex mill town.

Morzine has strived to keep its typical Savoyard character and culture, whilst also opening its doors to the tourist industry. The Savoyard strength, warmth and character is passed down from generation to generation. Morzine's true Savoyard culture has also preserved its agro-pastoral way of life, serving up typical tasty traditional Haute Savoyarde meals, mountain traditions and patois still spoken by real Morzinois.
